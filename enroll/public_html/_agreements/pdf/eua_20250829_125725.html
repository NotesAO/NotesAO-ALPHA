    We understand the sensitive nature of the data stored in NotesAO and take security seriously. We implement the following security and compliance measures:

    SSL Encryption: All data transmitted between your device (web browser) and the NotesAO servers is encrypted using Secure Socket Layer (SSL) or equivalent strong encryption (HTTPS). We will enter into a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) with your clinic if required under HIPAA or otherwise ensure that our services comply with HIPAA regulations for protecting patient health information.

    No Public Access: NotesAO’s system is closed to the public. Only users with valid credentials issued by NotesAO can access the platform. There are no public-facing interfaces that allow browsing of clinic data without authentication. This reduces exposure to unauthorized access.

    Audit Logs: The Service maintains logs of user activities (such as logins, data entries, and edits) for security, audit, and compliance purposes. These logs help in monitoring suspicious activities and can be used to trace any unauthorized or inappropriate access, should it occur.

    Compliance with Laws: NotesAO and its users must comply with all applicable laws and regulations regarding data privacy and security. This includes U.S. healthcare privacy laws such as HIPAA.     We retain personal data only for as long as it is necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law or by agreement with you.     Termination of Service: If your clinic’s subscription ends (either by cancellation or termination), we will typically retain your data in our systems for a limited grace period after termination – often 30 to 60 days – to allow for data export or account reactivation if needed. During this period, your account may be deactivated, but the data is not yet deleted. Upon your request, we can assist with exporting data in a generic format (e.g., CSV reports or PDF documents of reports) for your records.

    Deletion After Termination: After the grace period post-termination, we will proceed to delete or anonymize the personal data associated with your account in our production systems. This usually involves permanently deleting database records for participants and users and wiping or anonymizing any residual data in logs that can identify your clinic or individuals. We may retain some minimal information for internal recordkeeping such as the fact of your organization’s name, the dates of service, and billing records, but not the detailed client personal data.

    Backup Retention: Our backup files that contain personal data (from either active or terminated accounts) are retained for a certain rotation period (for example, backups might be kept for up to 90 days before being overwritten or deleted). Thus, it is possible that after we delete data from our live database, it could remain in encrypted backups for a brief period until those backups cycle out. We store backups securely, and if we must restore data from a backup for disaster recovery, we will re-delete any data for terminated accounts that should have been purged.

    Legal Requirements for Retention: In some cases, we might be required by law to retain certain data for a longer period. For instance, if a law enforcement request or legal hold is in place related to your data, we will retain the data as directed. Or, if relevant laws mandate that certain transaction logs be kept (for example, under financial regulations or specific healthcare statutes), we comply with those requirements. Additionally, as a Business Associate under HIPAA, we may be required to return or destroy PHI at the end of our relationship unless retention is required by law. If destruction is infeasible (e.g., stored in backups), we will extend protections to that data and prevent further use.

    Aggregated/Anonymized Data: Any aggregated analytics data that does not identify individuals or your organization may be retained indefinitely for our use (e.g., system performance metrics, generic usage statistics). This data no longer has personal identifiers.

    Ongoing Programs: If your clinic re-subscribes or maintains certain continuing programs, we can retain historical data as needed. By default, we assume continuous retention during active use; we only purge after a clear termination or at your specific request.

    We aim to be flexible: if you have specific retention needs or policies (for example, you want us to delete certain records earlier, or conversely you need data retained longer for compliance reasons), please contact us to discuss and we will try to accommodate reasonable requests, possibly under an addendum or custom agreement.
